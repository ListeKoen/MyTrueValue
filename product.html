My True Value is your personal app for self-development.

It teaches you how to value yourself every day!

It makes you feel loved for who you are.

It helps you build healthy self-worth and self-esteem through reflection and concrete tools. It teaches you self-care together with self-love and increases your feeling of love and happiness.

How does the app help you

My True Value is your guide with concrete exercises. It is exercises chosen by psychologists and therapists to help you understand yourself better and to gain healthy self-worth and self-esteem.

You will work on self-understanding, self-love, and self-care.

Exercises will contain reflection, concrete self-care practices, and self-love tools.

Self-care is a big part of your journey, and you will get to work on self-care every day.

The purpose is to make you feel good about yourself. No judging, just being and understanding with curiosity. It will be your private space.

Self-Worth vs. Self-esteem

Self-Esteem is what you think, feel, and believe about yourself. Your self-esteem increases automatically when working on your self-worth.

Self-worth is being able to feel good about yourself, to feel worthy, and to feel loved.

It is something that most people (including highly successful people) struggle with.

Self-worth has a direct influence on your mental health, happiness, and self-confidence.

A high sense of self-esteem and own worth affects all aspects of your life

It affects you in partnership, friendship, parenting, work-life, school-life, and decision making. It affects your well-being, your mental health, and your way of thinking.

When you feel loved, valuable, precious, and irreplaceable, it gives you room to be you. It leaves room to be the best version of you. Altogether, you will experience more joy and happiness in your life.

Bonus: Strong Mental Health

Self-care has in itself been proved to increase your feeling of happiness and mental health.

But working on both self-care and self-worth seems to shield us from many types of mental illness and emotional problems. Low self-worth has been identified as a factor in stress, addictions, depression, anxiety, and relationship issues.

Stress, depression, anxiety, and other mental conditions are often related to people, who deal with low self-worth and low self-esteem.

At this point, no one knows whether it's the mental condition and the stigma associated with it that causes the low self-worth or whether it happens the other way around. Either way, they build on each other, creating a persistent feeling of unworthiness and despair.

With a sense of low self-worth, you tend to get into unhealthy relations

If you have low self-worth, you tend to search for something in your relationships based on your own opinion of yourself. It affects all of your relationships, for example relationships with relatives, your kids, your friends, and your partner.


There are many reasons to work on your self-worth.
